Why Stingless Bees Matter

Ecological Importance

Stingless bees are crucial pollinators for many wild plants and crops, especially in tropical and subtropical regions, helping maintain biodiversity.

Medicinal Honey

The honey produced by stingless bees has unique properties and has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial benefits.

Cultural Significance

Many indigenous cultures have deep connections with stingless bees, incorporating them into traditions, myths, and sustainable beekeeping practices.

About Stingless Bees

Stingless bees (Meliponini) are a diverse group of highly social bees found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. Unlike their honey bee relatives, they lack functional stingers, making them especially suitable for urban and community beekeeping.

With over 500 species identified, these remarkable insects have evolved unique behaviors and nest structures.

Popular Stingless Bee Species

Tetragonula carbonaria

Native to Australia, these bees create distinctive spiral brood combs and are popular in backyard beekeeping.

Melipona beecheii

Known as Xunan Kab to the Maya people, these bees have been kept for thousands of years in Central America.

Trigona hyalinata

This South American species is known for creating large colonies and complex defensive behaviors.
